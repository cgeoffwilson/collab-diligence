What Straia Does

Straia is the agentic AI platform for higher education administration. It integrates with legacy systems of record — SIS (Banner, Colleague, Workday), LMS (Canvas, Blackboard, D2L), and CRM (Element451, Slate, EAB) — to create a unified data layer that allows any administrator to query, visualize, and act on institutional data in real time, without writing SQL or waiting weeks for institutional research.

The GTM wedge is a dual-track motion: sell both individual institutions (bottom-up) and state system leadership (top-down) simultaneously, then expand system-wide with minimal incremental integration work.

Their procurement hack: onboarding with Carahsoft to get listed on E&I cooperative buying contracts — bypassing the 6–12 month public RFP process entirely.

GTM Motion
01 Dual-Track System Selling
▶

Straia sells bottom-up (individual institution) and top-down (system chancellor/VP) simultaneously. When both sides see the value, system-wide deals close faster. This was demonstrated with TBR: Roane State was the beach-head, and system-level visibility became the hook for all 36 colleges.

Community colleges are organized in state systems (TN has 36 under one governing body, SUNY has 60+). Once one integration is built, expansion across the system requires minimal incremental work — shared SIS/LMS/CRM stacks.

02 Carahsoft / E&I Cooperative Procurement
▶

The biggest friction in selling to public higher ed is the RFP process — typically 6–12 months. Straia is in final stages of signing with Carahsoft, the largest government tech reseller in the US, to get listed on E&I Cooperative Services contracts.

Once listed, any public institution in a participating state can purchase directly without an RFP. This is a genuine procurement moat — not just a competitive advantage but a category unlocking mechanism. The combination of system-level selling and cooperative procurement means they can close a 36-institution deal without 36 separate RFPs.

03 Tennessee Proof Point
▶

Six months of relationship-building with the Tennessee Board of Regents (TBR), meeting every 2 months. Launched beta at Roane State CC. TBR is now accelerating Straia deployment across all 36 colleges — representing a $4.5M annual contract. Ryan and Alan (co-founders) lived on the Roane State campus for 3 months.

The TBR Chancellor's letter (Flora W. Tydings, Ed.D.) and Roane State President's letter (Chris Waley) both confirm system enthusiasm. 5.2 queries per user per week in beta Week 3, up 53% WoW — this is real usage, not demos.

04 Pricing Model
▶

Per-FTE pricing, tiered by institution size. Budget comes from enterprise IT — a horizontal tool accessible to the full administrative staff vs. legacy tools that serve 2-3 data analysts. Sample: a community college paying $70K/yr for legacy reporting tools → Straia at ~$150K/yr, deployed institution-wide.

Integration cost: $40–80K one-time (waived or reduced for early customers). Annual ARR: ranges from ~$90K (small 2-yr college) to $720K+ (Northern Virginia CC, 76K students). System contracts like TBR and SUNY are $1.7M–$9M+ ARR.

Carahsoft reseller take rate: ~10%. Net ARR is Carahsoft-adjusted across all pipeline.

Deep Dive Risk Analysis
HIGH Revenue Concentration (TBR Single Counterparty)
▶

The $3.8M in Procurement-stage pipeline is essentially one deal: TBR Community Colleges ($1.73M) and TBR TCAT ($2.07M). Both are with the same governing body — the Tennessee Board of Regents. A TBR procurement delay, budget freeze, or change in chancellor could materially impact near-term ARR.

Mitigant: The TBR Chancellor's and Roane State President's letters are strong social proof. The relationship has been built over 6+ months. The product is live and producing usage. But the contractual commitment should be confirmed — the TBR letter says "strong interest in expanding" not "signed contract."

Diligence question: Is the TBR expansion a signed contract or a letter of intent? What is the payment structure and trigger conditions?

HIGH Financial Model: Raise vs. Cost Gap
▶

The cost model totals $11.67M over 21 months (Apr 2026 – Dec 2027). The raise is $5–7M. The gap requires ~$4.7–6.7M in net revenue over the period, or a subsequent raise. The model's revenue targets ($9M ARR by 18 months) are aggressive — they require closing TBR + 2-3 additional systems. Missing by even one system creates meaningful cash pressure.

Key pressure point: April 2027 is when 7 new regional system coordinators onboard simultaneously, for SUNY, VA, IL, and KY expansion. Monthly burn jumps from ~$600K to ~$715K at that point. If those systems are still in "Lead" stage at that time, the company burns through cash rapidly.

Diligence question: What does the 12-month cash plan look like at $3M ARR vs $9M ARR? What is the next round plan?

HIGH Sales Cycle Velocity at Scale
▶

The Tennessee deal took 6+ months of relationship-building to reach procurement. Even with the Carahsoft/E&I bypass, large system deals (SUNY at $9M ARR, MN State at $4.9M) will require extensive stakeholder management, IT reviews, legal, and data governance approvals. The pipeline has 5 "Lead" stage system deals all projected to close in Feb–Nov 2027. That's 5 complex 8-figure TCV deals in 10 months.

Mitigant: System-level demand is genuine — TBR's success creates social proof for other chancellors. Carahsoft gets them past formal procurement. But the diligence, negotiation, and onboarding layers remain.

Diligence question: How many institutions have Straia formally onboarded to date? What does a full onboarding cycle look like (data dictionary buildout, FERPA compliance review, IT security review)?

MED Budget Cuts Hitting Community Colleges
▶

The deck's "Why Now" thesis notes $12B in education grants disrupted and 15 states cutting funding. This creates urgency — but also constrains IT budgets. Community colleges pricing at $100–150K/yr competing for the same budget as legacy reporting tools is defensible. But if states freeze IT procurement (as some are under current federal budget pressures), even a Carahsoft-listed vendor can get blocked.

Mitigant: Straia displaces existing spend (legacy reporting tools) rather than being incremental budget. The ROI case is clear. But timing and advocacy matters.

Diligence question: How are TBR and other active prospects navigating current state budget pressures? Have any conversations stalled for budget reasons?

MED Carahsoft / E&I Contract Not Yet Signed
▶

The GTM memo says they are "in final stages of signing" with Carahsoft and expect to be listed "within weeks." This is a significant piece of the GTM thesis — but it is not closed. Until they're on the cooperative contract, they face the full RFP process for any public institution. The procurement moat is partially hypothetical.

Diligence question: Can we get confirmation on Carahsoft timeline? Has the contract been signed? Is there an exclusivity or preferred-vendor arrangement?

Executive Summary

Straia is building the agentic AI operating system for higher education administration. In a market where hundreds of decisions are made on intuition — because getting a data query answered by institutional research takes one to two months — Straia gives any administrator with the right access the ability to query, visualize, and act on institutional data in real time.

The company launched its beta three weeks ago and went from zero to $675K ARR. It has a signed or near-signed deal with the Tennessee Board of Regents to deploy across all 36 community and technical colleges in the state — a $4.5M annual contract. The founding team is exceptional: a CEO who cold-called 3,000 colleges and lived on a campus for three months, a COO who built the commercial motion at Sana through its $1.1B Workday acquisition, a CTO with data infrastructure credentials at IPO-scale companies, and a CPO who has spent her career building complex products for non-technical users.

This is one of the strongest early-stage signals we have seen in the education technology space. We recommend proceeding to full diligence and moving quickly given the fundraise velocity.

Why This Matters for Collab

Collab's thesis — good for you and good for the world — fits Straia precisely. Community colleges are the institutions that serve as the first rung of opportunity for the largest and least-served population of American learners. They are the schools that gave Ryan's parents — immigrants from Hong Kong — a path to PhDs and a middle-class life. They are fighting for survival: enrollment declining, funding being cut from both state budgets and federal grants, administrative costs growing at 5x the rate of instructional staff.

Straia is not an incremental improvement. It is a platform that allows community colleges to make data-informed decisions for the first time — and ultimately to automate the administrative layer that has become so bloated, freeing resources for actual instruction and student support. The good for the world case is genuine: better student retention, earlier intervention for at-risk students, more efficient allocation of scarce resources.

We also have personal context. Craig ran entrepreneurship and innovation at NYU for 10 years and still teaches. His wife is a Columbia professor. We understand how broken higher ed administration is. We understand why this product resonates immediately with anyone who has worked inside a university.

The Opportunity

Higher education is at a structural inflection point. Enrollment faces a 15% decline over the next four years from the demographic cliff. $12B in federal education grants are disrupted. 15 states are cutting higher ed funding. Administrative staff has grown 5x faster than instructional staff over the last two decades. Every decision about enrollment, retention, curriculum, financial aid, and resource allocation is being made with 1–2 month old data, or on pure intuition, because the systems of record — Ellucian Banner, Canvas, Element451 — are siloed, legacy, and inaccessible without SQL.

The community college system structure creates a distribution mechanism that is unique to this market. Most colleges sit within state systems (Tennessee has 36 under one governing body, SUNY has 60+). Once Straia integrates with the common SIS/LMS/CRM stack at one institution, it can expand system-wide with minimal incremental work. System-level leaders have no real-time visibility into what is happening across their institutions — Straia solves this with a unified intelligence dashboard that gives chancellors the cross-system view they have never had.

Straia's beachhead is the ~1,600 community and technical colleges in state systems across the US, representing a $425M serviceable addressable market. This expands to $2.25B with 4-year colleges and to $12.5B internationally.

Product & Differentiation

The product is a unified agentic layer that integrates with SIS (Banner, Colleague, Workday, PeopleSoft), LMS (Blackboard, Canvas, D2L), and CRM (Element451, Slate, EAB Navigate) systems. The core technical differentiation is the context layer — a school-specific data dictionary that maps institutional terminology and data definitions before any query is run. This is the moat: every institution names and defines its data differently, and building this context takes months per institution. Straia has already done it.

Phase 1 (now): Unified query and visualization — any administrator, role-based access, real-time. 5.2 queries per user per week in beta Week 3, growing 53% WoW. Phase 1.5 (building now): triggers and interventions — early warning systems and automated outreach to at-risk students directly within the platform. Long-term vision: the full administrative operating system for higher education, automating every workflow outside of instruction.

Competitive positioning: Straia is the only platform that is both enterprise-wide (not a point solution) and higher-ed native (not a generic tool like Glean or Copilot). Civitas Learning, HelioCampus, and EAB are higher-ed native but point solutions with no agentic layer.
