What Straia Does

Straia is the agentic AI platform for higher education administration. It integrates with legacy SIS, LMS, and CRM systems to create a unified data layer that allows any administrator to query, visualize, and act on institutional data in real time — without SQL or waiting weeks for institutional research.

Their GTM wedge: a dual-track motion — sell both individual institutions (bottom-up) and state system leadership (top-down) simultaneously, then expand system-wide with minimal incremental integration work.

Procurement hack: onboarding with Carahsoft and E&I cooperative buying contracts — bypassing the 6–12 month public RFP process entirely.

GTM Motion
01Dual-Track System Selling
▶

Straia sells bottom-up (individual institution) and top-down (system chancellor/VP) simultaneously. When both sides close, system-wide deals accelerate. This was demonstrated with TBR: Roane State was the beachhead, and system-level visibility became the hook for all 36 colleges.

Community colleges are organized in state systems (TN has 36 under one governing body, SUNY has 60+). Once one integration is built, expansion requires minimal incremental work — shared SIS/LMS/CRM stacks throughout.

02Carahsoft / E&I Cooperative Procurement
▶

The biggest friction in selling to public higher ed is the RFP process — typically 6–12 months. Straia is in final stages of signing with Carahsoft, the largest government tech reseller in the US, to get listed on E&I Cooperative Services contracts. Once listed, any public institution can purchase directly without an RFP.

Note: contract not yet signed as of April 2026 — confirm status immediately.

03Tennessee Proof Point
▶

Six months of relationship-building with Tennessee Board of Regents (TBR), meeting every 2 months. Launched beta at Roane State CC. TBR is now accelerating Straia deployment across all 36 colleges — representing a $4.5M annual contract. Ryan and Alan lived on the Roane State campus for 3 months.

5.2 queries per user per week in beta Week 3, up 53% WoW — real usage, not demos. TBR Chancellor and Roane State President have both written strong endorsement letters.

04Pricing Model
▶

Per-FTE pricing, tiered by institution size. Budget comes from enterprise IT. Replaces legacy reporting tools accessible only to 2–3 data analysts — Straia is deployed institution-wide. A college paying $70K/yr for legacy tools → Straia at ~$150K/yr, full admin access.

Integration cost: $40–80K one-time (waived for early customers). Carahsoft reseller take rate: ~10%. System contracts range from $1.7M–$9M+ ARR.

Executive Summary

Straia is building the agentic AI operating system for higher education administration. In a market where hundreds of decisions are made on intuition — because getting a data query answered by institutional research takes one to two months — Straia gives any administrator the ability to query, visualize, and act on institutional data in real time.

The company launched its beta three weeks ago and went from zero to $675K ARR. It has a deal in advanced stages with the Tennessee Board of Regents to deploy across all 36 community and technical colleges — a $4.5M annual contract. The founding team is exceptional: a CEO who cold-called 3,000 colleges and lived on campus for three months, a COO who built the commercial motion at Sana through its $1.1B Workday acquisition, a CTO with data infrastructure credentials at IPO-scale companies, and a CPO who has spent her career building complex products for non-technical users.

This is one of the strongest early-stage signals we have seen in education technology. We recommend proceeding to full diligence and moving quickly given the fundraise velocity.

Why This Matters for Collab

Collab's thesis — good for you and good for the world — fits Straia precisely. Community colleges are the institutions that serve as the first rung of opportunity for the largest and least-served population of American learners. They are fighting for survival: enrollment declining, funding being cut, administrative costs growing at 5x the rate of instructional staff.

Straia is not an incremental improvement. It is a platform that allows community colleges to make data-informed decisions for the first time — and ultimately to automate the administrative layer, freeing resources for actual instruction and student support. The good for the world case is genuine: better student retention, earlier intervention for at-risk students, more efficient allocation of scarce resources.

We have personal context. Craig ran entrepreneurship and innovation at NYU for 10 years and still teaches. His wife is a Columbia professor. We understand how broken higher ed administration is and why this product resonates immediately with anyone who has worked inside a university.

The Opportunity

Higher education is at a structural inflection point. Enrollment faces a 15% decline over the next four years. $12B in federal education grants are disrupted. Administrative staff has grown 5x faster than instructional staff over the last two decades. Every decision is being made with 1–2 month old data because the legacy systems — Ellucian Banner, Canvas, Element451 — are siloed and inaccessible without SQL.

The community college system structure creates a distribution mechanism unique to this market. Most colleges sit within state systems (Tennessee has 36, SUNY has 60+). Once Straia integrates with the common stack at one institution, it can expand system-wide with minimal incremental work. System-level leaders have no real-time cross-institution visibility — Straia solves this with a unified intelligence dashboard.

Beachhead: ~1,600 community and technical colleges in state systems across the US, representing a $425M SAM. Expands to $2.25B with 4-year colleges and $12.5B internationally.

Product & Differentiation

The core technical differentiation is the context layer — a school-specific data dictionary that maps institutional terminology before any query is run. This is the moat: every institution names and defines its data differently, and building this context takes months per institution. Straia has already done it.

Phase 1 (now): unified query and visualization. Phase 1.5 (building now): triggers and interventions — early warning systems and automated outreach to at-risk students. Long-term vision: the full administrative operating system for higher education.

Competitive positioning: Straia is the only platform that is both enterprise-wide and higher-ed native. Civitas Learning, HelioCampus, and EAB are higher-ed native but point solutions with no agentic layer. Glean and Copilot are enterprise-wide but generic.

This is a complete founding team with directly relevant experience across every dimension of the challenge ahead.
